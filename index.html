About

Our goal is to provide support and resources for individuals with non-visible disabilities, and to raise awareness about the challenges and obstacles these people face. We strive to promote inclusivity and accessibility in all aspects of society, and to empower people with non-visible disabilities to live their lives to the fullest. We believe that everyone should have the opportunity to thrive and reach their full potential, regardless of any challenges they may face. To support this goal, we seek to provide a range of services and resources to help individuals with non-visible disabilities receive the help they need. These may include support groups, online forums, informational resources, and referral services to connect individuals with relevant organizations and support networks. We also work to raise awareness about the rights and accommodations available to people with non-visible disabilities, and to advocate for increased access and support in all areas of society, especially in areas of education and life at home.

Non-Visible Disabilities

People with non-visible disabilities, such as learning disabilities, mental illness, and chronic pain, may face unique challenges in their daily lives. For example, a person with a learning disability may have difficulty accessing education and may require specialized support in order to succeed in school. A person with mental illness may struggle with stigma and discrimination, and may have difficulty finding and maintaining employment. A person with chronic pain may face barriers to accessing healthcare and may require accommodations in order to participate fully in daily activities. Overall, non-visible disabilities can present a range of complex challenges that can affect every aspect of a person's life.

Non-Visible Disabilities Within The Classroom

It is important to recognize the importance of providing accommodations for those with disabilities within a classroom. This can include providing extra time for tests, providing alternative seating arrangements, or providing access to assistive technology. These accommodations can help to ensure that students with disabilities are able to participate in the classroom and have the same opportunities as their peers. It is also important to recognize the importance of providing support for those with disabilities. This can include providing a safe space for students to talk about their experiences, providing resources for students to access, and providing support for students to help them cope with their disability that might not be apparent to the naked eye. This can help to ensure that students with disabilities are able to feel comfortable and included in the classroom. We also must recognize the importance of providing resources for those with disabilities. This can include providing access to support groups, providing access to counseling services, and providing access to assistive technology. These resources can help to ensure that students with disabilities are able to access the support they need to succeed in the classroom. Overall, it is ideal to recognize the disabilities in society that one may not be able to see on its own. This can help to ensure that students with disabilities are able to feel comfortable and included in the classroom, and that they are able to access the resources they need to succeed. By recognizing these disabilities, we can help to create a more inclusive and supportive learning environment for all students.

ARISE Impact

When it comes to disabilities within people, especially disabilities that cannot be seen with the naked eye, people often forget to think about how it makes the person feel. The only thing people tend to think about is how a disability can make someone “different” and how it can make their life more difficult to live. The way a disability can not only affect someone directly, but also affect them by damaging their self esteem is often overlooked. Many people with these kinds of disabilities often struggle socially even if the disability they have has no effect on their social skills. The reason they struggle socially is solely because of the fact that they are super insecure and self conscious because they know they have a disability, even though the disability itself has no effect on their social abilities. These struggles may lead to depression, anxiety, and other problems within those with these kinds of disabilities, and makes their lives harder than it has to be. Luckily, companies like ARISE Impact is on a mission to create opportunities and form modules so that people with disabilities can establish their confidence and develop new learning tactics. If you want to make a difference, you can contact ARISE Impact at contact@ariseimpact.org, or you can visit their website at http://www.ariseimpact.org/ to learn more!
